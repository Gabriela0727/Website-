2024 — Data Analytics Project: Economic Recessions & Music Trends

Investigated correlations between U.S. economic downturns (2000–2023) and popular music characteristics using Billboard Hot 100 and Spotify datasets (3,397 songs).
Extracted and analyzed audio features (valence, energy, danceability) and lyrical sentiment using Python and statistical tools.
Integrated macroeconomic data from FRED (GDP, unemployment, consumer confidence) to identify patterns in music during recession vs. non-recession periods.
Found weak but suggestive links: during recessions, music showed slightly more positive sentiment and a shift toward upbeat genres like dance/pop.
Visualized trends over time with regression models, sentiment density plots, and genre distribution charts; observed notable genre shifts and emotional responses around economic transitions.
Demonstrated music’s potential role as both cultural reflection and emotional escape during economic hardship.
